## Peer-Reviewed Papers

### 1. Indian Hajj Medical Mission Study (2014–2016)

Khan, I.D., Khan, S.A., Asima, B., Hussaini, S.B., Zakiuddin, M., & Faisal, F.A. (2018). Morbidity and mortality amongst Indian Hajj pilgrims: A 3-year experience of Indian Hajj medical mission in mass-gathering medicine. *Journal of Infection and Public Health*, 11(2), 165–170.

https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jiph.2017.06.004

**Protocols informed:** Heat Stroke, Heat Exhaustion, Heart Attack, Severe Pneumonia, Acute Asthma Attack, COPD Exacerbation, Hypoglycemia, Severe Dehydration, Fracture, Ankle Sprain, Crush Injuries, Acute Gastroenteritis, Diabetic Foot Infection, Muscle Cramps, Skin Irritation

**Key findings used:**
- Infectious diseases 53.26% of all cases
- Respiratory infections 49.42% of outpatient cases
- Orthopaedic and trauma 24.40% of cases
- 45% of fractures were Colle's fractures from falls on outstretched hand
- Crush injuries from crowd overstepping documented
- Diabetic foot and cellulitis as common presentations
- Crude mortality 11.99 per 10,000 pilgrims (2016)

### 2. Cardiovascular Disease in Hajj Pilgrims

Al Shimemeri, A. (2012). Cardiovascular disease in Hajj pilgrims. *Journal of the Saudi Heart Association*, 24(2), 123–127.

https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jsha.2012.02.004

**Protocols informed:** Heart Attack, Fainting / Syncope

**Key findings used:**
- Myocardial infarction major cause of ICU admission during Hajj
- More than 60% of ICU admissions from cardiovascular causes
- Cardiovascular disease caused 45.8% of deaths during 2002 Hajj
- Up to half of recorded deaths from myocardial infarction alone
- Age-mortality correlation: 67.5% of deaths in patients aged 60–79

### 3. Health Risk Behaviors Among Hajj 2024 Pilgrims

Samarkandi, O., Alamri, F.A., Alhazmi, J., Alsaleh, G.S., Alsehdwei, K.A., Alabdullatif, L., Alazmy, W., & Khan, A. (2025). Health Risk Behaviors and Associated Factors Among Hajj 2024 Pilgrims: A Multinational Cross-Sectional Study. *Risk Management and Healthcare Policy*, 18, 2233–2245.

https://doi.org/10.2147/RMHP.S521097

**Protocols informed:** Heat Stroke, Heat Exhaustion, Severe Dehydration, Fracture, Crush Injuries

**Key findings used:**
- 26.9% of pilgrims never used umbrella under direct sunlight
- 49.3% walked in hot direct sunlight rather than using available transport
- 51.7% did not use prescribed medications during Hajj
- Only 40.1% requested urgent health assistance with severe symptoms
- Muscular injuries and fractures attributed to excessive walking of 5–15 miles
- Saudi Arabia temperatures range from 27°C to 43°C during Hajj season
- Mean pilgrim age 54.98 years; 40.4% had chronic diseases
- Hypertension 61.6%, Diabetes 47.7%, Asthma 14.2% among pilgrims with chronic disease

### 4. Respiratory Illness Among Malaysian Pilgrims (2013)

Hashim, S., Ayub, Z.N., Mohamed, Z., Hasan, H., Harun, A., Ismail, N., Rahman, Z.A., Suraiya, S., Naing, N.N., & Aziz, A.A. (2016). The prevalence and preventive measures of the respiratory illness among Malaysian pilgrims in 2013 Hajj season. *Journal of Travel Medicine*, 23(2), tav019.

https://doi.org/10.1093/jtm/tav019

**Protocols informed:** Severe Pneumonia, Acute Asthma Attack, COPD Exacerbation

**Key findings used:**
- Overall respiratory illness prevalence 93.4% among Malaysian pilgrims
- 78.2% fulfilled criteria for influenza-like illness
- 81.2% acquired infection after brief stay at Arafat
- 61.8% required antibiotic treatment
- Only 2.1% hospitalized
- Contact with ill pilgrims: OR 2.61 for higher risk
- Good hand hygiene: OR 0.35 for lower risk
- Crowd densities during Hajj up to 7 people per square metre

### 5. Mass Gatherings and Public Health: Case Studies from the Hajj (2015)

Rahman, J., Thu, M., Arshad, N., & Van der Putten, M. (2017). Mass Gatherings and Public Health: Case Studies from the Hajj to Mecca. *Annals of Global Health*, 83(2), 386–393.

https://doi.org/10.1016/j.aogh.2016.12.001

**Protocols informed:** Heat Stroke, Heat Exhaustion, Crush Injuries, Fracture, Skin Irritation

**Key findings used:**
- Temperature in Arafat documented at approximately 55°C during 2015 Hajj
- Pilgrims suffered heat exhaustion, back pain, hypertension exacerbation at Arafat
- One groupmate died from heat stroke at Arafat
- Stampede at Jamarat during stoning of Satan documented
- Crowd density near Kaaba: 6–8 people per square metre; certain locations: 12 per square metre
- Wheelchair Achilles tendon injuries from pedestrian-wheelchair mixing documented
- Average daytime temperatures exceed 38°C during Hajj season
- Body temperature of 40.0°C: one person may walk to clinic while another dies from heat stroke

## Official Guidelines

### 6. Saudi Ministry of Health / Nusuk Hajj Health Guidelines

Saudi Ministry of Health. (2026). Hajj Health Guidelines. Nusuk Hajj Platform.

https://hajj.nusuk.sa/nusuk/health/guidelines

Accessed: January 2026.

**Protocols informed:** Heat Stroke, Heat Exhaustion, Severe Dehydration, Nosebleed, Hypoglycemia, Skin Irritation, Ankle Sprain, Acute Gastroenteritis, Muscle Cramps

**Key content used:**
- Official heat stroke first aid steps
- Dehydration signs and prevention
- Nosebleed management: pinch soft part, lean forward, 5–10 minutes
- Low blood sugar: fast-acting sugar, 15-minute wait, unconscious = emergency
- Ankle sprain: RICE protocol, ice 15–20 minutes
- Food poisoning prevention and management
- Emergency contacts: 937 (Pilgrims Call Center), 977 (Red Crescent), 999 (Police)

## Note on Knowledge Base Construction

All protocol content in Hayat AI was written by the author based on clinical information from the above sources. No protocol content was copied verbatim from the papers. Each protocol was cross-referenced against at least one source before inclusion.

The evaluation test set (52 queries in `hayat_evaluation.py`) was also constructed strictly from documented cases and clinical presentations in these papers - no hallucinated symptoms or invented scenarios were used.
